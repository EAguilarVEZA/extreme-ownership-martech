Extreme Ownership: a martech curriculum

A self-paced learning path inspired by the leadership principles popularized in Extreme Ownership by Jocko Willink and Leif Babin — translated into how a healthcare martech team actually operates across web, CRM, email, data, and reporting.

This is not a reproduction of the book. It walks the team through all twelve principles, chapter by chapter, in plain language — no prior reading required.

Own the mission. Own the gaps. Own the handoff.

Extreme ownership means the team stops asking "who dropped this?" and starts asking "what did we miss, what will we change, and who owns the next action?"

How to use this

Each chapter gives you the core idea, what it looks like on our team, and one exercise you can finish in a few minutes. Then make it real in the practice lab at the end.

    After-action review
    Run an after-action review on [project].
1. What was supposed to happen?
2. What actually happened?
3. What did we own too late?
4. What one system or process change prevents a repeat?
5. Who owns that change, by when?
    
            
    Commander's intent
    Write the commander's intent for [initiative]:
- The mission in one sentence
- Why it matters (the "why")
- The end state that means "done"
- The boundaries owners can act within without asking
